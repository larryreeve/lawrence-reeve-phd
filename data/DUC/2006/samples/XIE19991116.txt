<HEADLINE> Sinn Fein Pledges Not to Use Force for Political Purposes </HEADLINE>
<TEXT>
<P>
LONDON, November 16 (Xinhua) -- The Sinn Fein, the political
wing of the Irish Republican Army, pledged Tuesday to oppose the
use of force for political purposes.
</P>
<P>
It said in a statement issued in Belfast that disarmament was
an essential part of Northern Ireland's peace process which could
be achieved with the help of the authorities in charge of arms
decommissioning in Northern Ireland.
</P>
<P>
"Sinn Fein accepts that disarmament is an essential part of the
peace process," said Sinn Fein leader Gerry Adams.
</P>
<P>
"We believe that the issue of arms will be finally and
satisfactorily settled under the aegis of the de Chastelain (
disarmament) commission as set out in the agreement," Adams said
in a statement.
</P>
<P>
He added that the Sinn Fein was totally committed to peace and
democracy and was definitely opposed to any use of force or threat
of force for any political purpose. 
</P>
